Maithli Thakur

Maithili is just 22 years old Indian singer as of 2022. She is an Indian playback singer and also trained in Indian classical and Folk Music. Maithili has mainly performed in Hindi, Bengali, Maithili, Urdu, Marathi, Bhojpuri, Punjabi, Tamil, English, and other Indic languages. She sings in various categories, especially original songs, covers, and traditional folk music. Maithili is also active on YouTube. She has a YouTube channel with above 2.58 million subscribers.Indian classical vocalist Maithili Thakur has around 2.3 million Instagram followers. She often updates her Instagram account with details about her singing performances, music, concerts, and singing videos for her followers. Maithili was given a God-gifted talent to sing in a variety of languages, including Telugu, Tamil, Bhojpuri, Marathi, and Hindi. She performs a variety of Bollywood cover songs in addition to other state-specific traditional folk music. She can be seen with Rishav and Ayachi, her two younger brothers. Ayachi mostly sings, while Rishav plays the Tabla. Maithili Thakur and her two brothers were appointed as the Madhuban District's brand ambassadors by the Election Commission in 2019. She debuted with the album "Ya Rabba" and won the Indian singing competition "I-Genius Young Singing Star" in 2015 (Universal Music).

Bharti Singh

Comedian-host Bharti Singh shares that she has to leave her child at home for hours and hence not being compensated well irks her. Besides comedy shows and hosting, Bharti Singh’s focus lately has been her YouTube channel, and she’s enjoying working for herself. However, that’s not the only reason for her inclination towards her channel, as the comedian-host tells us that post Covid-19, budgets of the shows have suffered and in turn affected her remuneration.“Ever since the pandemic hit us, budgets of the shows have really been affected. And that has happened in all the industries. But, no artiste would be fine if they are not paid what they deserve. Agar main jo charge karti thi, uska 25% bhi nahi doge aap, toh fir kaam nahi ho ho payega,” says Singh, adding that she won’t mind if it’s just a day’s work.“But if you ask me to give 26 days in a month to a show, and I am not even being paid well for it, I would prefer to take a step back because main bhi apne bacche ko 12 hours ghar par chhod kar aaungi, so I need to be paid for my work,” she asserts.
